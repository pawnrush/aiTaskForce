The Evolution of a Prompt

BEFORE: Basic Prompt

"Give me IEP goals."

This vague request leads to generic, often unusable results that require heavy editing and lack specific context.

AFTER: Engineered Prompt

"[System] You are an expert SLP assistant. [User] Draft 3 SMART IEP goals for a 2nd grader with /r/ articulation errors..."

This precise instruction, using roles and context, produces a predictable, high-quality, and actionable draft, saving valuable time.

The Prompt Engineering Workflow

Follow these steps to build effective prompts that deliver predictable results every time, moving from a general idea to a specific, high-quality output.

Step 1: Set the System

Start by defining the AI's persona and global rules. This is optional but highly recommended for setting a consistent foundation.

Step 2: Define the User Request

Clearly state the persona, action, context, and constraints for your specific task. Be as detailed as possible.

Step 3: Provide an Example (Optional)

If the format is complex, use the Assistant role to "show" the AI exactly what you want. This is a powerful way to ensure stylistic consistency.

Step 4: Review and Refine

Critically evaluate the output. If it's not perfect, refine your User prompt with more specific keywords or clearer constraints.

Keywords: Your AI Control Panel

Beyond roles, specific keywords act as powerful directives. By telling the AI exactly how to format, what content to focus on, the desired tone, and the rules to follow, you gain granular control over the final product. This chart shows the categories of keywords available to you.

Using keywords like `Format as: table` or `Focus on: antecedent strategies` removes guesswork and ensures the AI's output is structured and focused precisely to your needs, turning it into a more reliable and efficient tool.
